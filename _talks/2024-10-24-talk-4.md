  <strong style="color:#e8f5e8;">Introduction to Nets and Filters in Topology</strong>, delivered at <strong style="color:#e8f5e8;">PhD Seminar Series, Department of Mathematics, IIIT Delhi</strong>, New Delhi, India, 2024.
<br><br> 
  <strong style="color:#e8f5e8;">Overview:</strong> The talk explored the generalization of sequences through <strong style="color:#e8f5e8;">nets</strong> and <strong style="color:#e8f5e8;">filters</strong>, and their role in capturing convergence and continuity in topological spaces where sequential notions are insufficient.
<br><br>
  <strong style="color:#e8f5e8;">Topics Covered:</strong>
  <ul style="color:#e8f5e8; margin-top: 5px;">
    <li>Motivation behind nets and filters</li>
    <li>Directed sets and their role in topology</li>
    <li>Convergence via nets and filters</li>
    <li>Applications in characterizing compactness and continuity</li>
  </ul>
  This seminar was aimed at a general audience.
