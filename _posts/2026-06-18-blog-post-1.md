  The Fan on the Wall

<div style="background:#0e2e0e; padding:18px 22px; border-radius:8px; color:#e8f5e8; font-family:Georgia, serif; font-size:16px; margin-bottom:20px; text-align:center;">

Maryam Mirzakhani Research Lab.

<br><br>

Seat D.

<br><br>

The fan on the wall keeps spinning.

<br><br>

Its motion is visible.

<br><br>

Mine is not.

<br><br>

The blades move in circles,

steady,

predictable,

unbothered.

<br><br>

Meanwhile,

my thoughts revolve faster than the fan itself.

<br><br>

Around the same questions.

<br><br>

What has changed in me?

<br><br>

How am I changing?

<br><br>

Have I grown,

or merely grown older?

<br><br>

Am I different from the person

who arrived here three years ago,

or am I still carrying

the same fears

under different circumstances?

<br><br><br>

The workplace has changed.

<br><br>

People have come and gone.

<br><br>

New notices cover old notices.

<br><br>

New students occupy old chairs.

<br><br>

The walls remember everything.

<br><br>

But when I look at myself,

I cannot measure the distance travelled.

<br><br>

Has there been intellectual growth?

<br><br>

Intellectual decay?

<br><br>

Or both,

happening together,

in proportions I cannot calculate?

<br><br><br>

Some days

I feel like the dumbest creature on Earth.

<br><br>

A creature carrying

beautiful motivations,

grand ambitions,

and elegant dreams.

<br><br>

Yet struggling

to transform thought

into action.

<br><br>

A collector of aspirations.

<br><br>

A poor executor of them.

<br><br><br>

Perhaps I know more

than I once did.

<br><br>

But perhaps I also know enough

to see the vastness

of what I do not know.

<br><br>

And maybe that is why

certainty disappears.

<br><br>

The horizon expands

faster than the journey.

<br><br><br>

The fan continues spinning.

<br><br>

Hour after hour.

<br><br>

Day after day.

<br><br>

Its progress is obvious.

<br><br>

Mine remains invisible.

<br><br>

No instrument measures

the slow transformation

of a human mind.

<br><br>

No theorem announces

that a person has changed.

<br><br>

No proof certifies

that growth has occurred.

<br><br><br>

Perhaps growth arrives

disguised as confusion.

<br><br>

Perhaps understanding begins

when certainty ends.

<br><br>

Perhaps the version of me

who thought he understood everything

had to disappear

so that another version

could begin learning honestly.

<br><br><br>

The fan keeps spinning.

<br><br>

The thoughts keep spinning faster.

<br><br>

And somewhere between the two,

unnoticed by both,

a life continues

to take shape.

<br><br>

Not finished.

<br><br>

Not understood.

<br><br>

Still becoming.

</div>
