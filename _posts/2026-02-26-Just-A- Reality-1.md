<h3><strong>The Donkey with Specs</strong></h3>

<p>
Once there was a herd of donkeys<br>
carrying loads up the same hill<br>
day after day after day.
</p>

<p>
They were told the hill was a mountain<br>
and the load was knowledge<br>
and the sweating was sacred.
</p>

<p>
Some donkeys carried more than others.<br>
Some fell.<br>
Some were told they did not try hard enough.
</p>

<p>
Then one donkey found a pair of spectacles<br>
lying on the path.<br>
It put them on<br>
and suddenly the other donkeys stopped.
</p>

<blockquote>
"That one sees differently," they whispered.<br>
"That one must be special."<br>
"That one should lead."
</blockquote>

<p>
The donkey with specs did not know more.<br>
It did not carry more.<br>
It just looked different while doing the same thing.
</p>

<p>
Soon the herd decided:<br>
whoever wears the specs<br>
wins the game.
</p>

<p>
So donkeys stopped looking at the road.<br>
They stopped asking where the hill led.<br>
They only searched for spectacles<br>
— any spectacles —<br>
to put on their faces.
</p>

<p>
The clever donkeys learned to polish old frames.<br>
The loud donkeys claimed they invented lenses.<br>
The tired donkeys kept their heads down<br>
and carried the load<br>
while the ones with specs<br>
stood on top<br>
calling it progress.
</p>

<p>
No one remembered anymore<br>
what the game was for.<br>
No one asked if the hill<br>
even needed climbing.
</p>

<p>
And the donkey with specs?<br>
It believed its own legend.<br>
It forgot it found the glasses by accident.<br>
It started telling the others:<br>
"Carry harder.<br>
Maybe one day<br>
you will find specs too."
</p>

<p>
But here is the truth the herd cannot hear:<br>
The hill goes nowhere.<br>
The load is the same for everyone.<br>
And spectacles<br>
do not help you see<br>
what was never there.
</p>

<p>
Somewhere underneath all that carrying,<br>
a few donkeys still remember:<br>
we came here to find water,<br>
not to win a game<br>
invented by the donkey<br>
who found glasses first.
</p>
