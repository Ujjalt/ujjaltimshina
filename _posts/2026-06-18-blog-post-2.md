  Diary of an Idle Man
</div>

<div style="background:#0e2e0e; padding:18px 22px; border-radius:8px; color:#e8f5e8; font-family:Georgia, serif; font-size:16px; margin-bottom:20px;">

<p>
It has been said by several people that I spend too much time in the lab.
</p>

<p>
That I sit there from morning until midnight.
</p>

<p>
That I do not play enough sports.
</p>

<p>
That a person cannot possibly remain productive from sunrise until the end of the day.
</p>

<p>
And then comes the question:
</p>

<blockquote style="border-left:3px solid #8ab68a; padding-left:15px; margin-left:10px; color:#d8ead8;">
What do you do all this time?
</blockquote>

<p>
Most of the time I remain quiet.
</p>

<p>
Not because I possess a profound answer.
</p>

<p>
Not because I disagree.
</p>

<p>
But because silence is often the most accurate answer available to me.
</p>

<p>
Many disappear into thoughts about life.
</p>

<p>
About time.
</p>

<p>
About memory.
</p>

<p>
About ambition.
</p>

<p>
About whether a person changes gradually or simply wakes up one day and notices that years have gone missing.
</p>

<p>
Sometimes the mind wanders elsewhere.
</p>

<p>
Toward questions that are neither mathematical nor practical.
</p>

<p>
Questions that have accompanied humanity far longer than any theorem.
</p>

<p>
Why is kindness so rare when its cost appears so small?
</p>

<p>
Why do people find it easier to divide themselves than to understand one another?
</p>

<p>
Why do simple things slowly become complicated?
</p>

<p>
And why do complicated things acquire guardians who ensure they remain that way?
</p>

<p>
Even in mathematics, I occasionally find myself wondering why certain simple observations are hidden beneath layers of notation, terminology, and tradition.
</p>

<p>
Not hidden deliberately, perhaps.
</p>

<p>
But hidden nonetheless.
</p>

<p>
As if every generation must rediscover parts of what has already been seen.
</p>

<p>
As if understanding sometimes travels less efficiently than knowledge itself.
</p>

<p>
Perhaps this struggle is necessary.
</p>

<p>
Perhaps every idea must be earned.
</p>

<p>
Or perhaps humanity occasionally makes difficult what was already difficult enough.
</p>

<p>
I do not know.
</p>

<p>
I only wonder whether a little more clarity, a little more patience, and a little more kindness would accelerate not only mathematics but human life itself.
</p>

<p>
Then again, these are merely the thoughts of an idle man.
</p>

<p>
And idle men have always been good at asking questions for which they possess no answers.
</p>

<p>
I agree with my critics more often than they might expect.
</p>

<p>
I am unproductive far more frequently than I would like.
</p>

<p>
Entire afternoons sometimes vanish without a theorem, without a proof, without a useful observation, and without anything I would confidently call progress.
</p>

<p>
Only a date remains behind.
</p>

<p>
A quiet witness on a calendar.
</p>

<p>
Had I written these thoughts in Nepali, perhaps they would have arrived more naturally.
</p>

<p>
The feelings know that language better than I do.
</p>

<p>
But then readers such as you would have to struggle through unfamiliar words.
</p>

<p>
So I search for English phrases instead.
</p>

<p>
Borrowing from books.
</p>

<p>
From memory.
</p>

<p>
From conversations.
</p>

<p>
From long evenings spent looking for a sentence capable of carrying a thought.
</p>

<p>
And thus another day passes.
</p>

<p>
Another page is written.
</p>

<p>
Another question remains unanswered.
</p>

<p>
And another entry quietly finds its place in the diary of an idle man.
</p>

</div>
