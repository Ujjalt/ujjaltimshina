<h3><strong>I Wish I Was a Dog</strong></h3>

<p>
I wish I was a dog<br>
not because life would be softer<br>
but because <strong>I</strong> would be simpler.
</p>

<p>
It is easy to say others pretend<br>
easy to point at noise<br>
and call it hollow<br>
but maybe it is my own voice<br>
that shakes a little<br>
when real truth is required.
</p>

<p>
Maybe I decorate my sentences too<br>
hoping the shine hides<br>
the part of me still unfinished.<br>
Maybe I walk into rooms<br>
with borrowed calm<br>
afraid my own quiet<br>
is not enough.
</p>

<blockquote>
I have performed certainty<br>
when I was not sure.<br>
I have smiled like understanding<br>
when I barely held the thread.<br>
And I have feared being seen<br>
without all the layers.
</blockquote>

<p>
A dog would not do that.<br>
A dog does not doubt<br>
the weight of its own presence.<br>
It does not rehearse honesty<br>
or polish its instincts.<br>
It does not hide behind cleverness<br>
when it feels small.
</p>

<p>
I wish I was a dog<br>
so I could stop holding myself<br>
to the impossible task<br>
of always seeming composed<br>
always seeming capable<br>
always seeming more<br>
than the trembling truth of me.
</p>

<p>
If simplicity were a choice<br>
I would choose it<br>
even for a single breath.
</p>
