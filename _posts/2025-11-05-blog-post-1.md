  This is a passing thought, written during my PhD journey. It is not an argument, nor a conclusion. It is simply a reflection on the lives of those who dedicate themselves to knowledge and discovery, often at a cost that remains unseen.
</div>

<div style="background:#0e2e0e; padding:18px 22px; border-radius:8px; color:#e8f5e8; font-family:Georgia, serif; font-size:22px; font-weight:bold; margin-bottom:20px; text-align:center;">
  Forgotten Minds
</div>

<div style="background:#0e2e0e; padding:18px 22px; border-radius:8px; color:#e8f5e8; font-family:Georgia, serif; font-size:16px; margin-bottom:20px; text-align:center;">

We build temples of knowledge,

<br><br>

but the worshippers grow weary inside.

<br><br>

We speak of progress,

<br><br>

while the minds that power it quietly fade.

<br><br>

In the name of excellence,

<br><br>

we trade curiosity for survival.

<br><br>

Innovation shines on paper,

<br><br>

but the dreamer's heart goes unread.

</div>

<div style="background:#0e2e0e; padding:18px 22px; border-radius:8px; color:#e8f5e8; font-family:Georgia, serif; font-size:16px; margin-bottom:20px;">

One day, I will uncover the bitter truth of the life walked by those who dare to pursue the path of innovation and research.

<br><br>

It is a path often chosen by individuals from the middle and lower classes, people driven not by comfort, but by courage, conviction, and the hope that knowledge can create a better future.

<br><br>

Their struggles are rarely documented. Their sacrifices seldom appear in research papers, university reports, or graduation speeches.

<br><br>

Yet behind every discovery lies a human story — a story of uncertainty, perseverance, loneliness, ambition, and belief.

</div>

<div style="background:#0e2e0e; padding:18px 22px; border-radius:8px; color:#e8f5e8; font-family:Georgia, serif; font-size:16px; margin-bottom:20px;">

I may be no one to speak for them now.

<br><br>

I am still walking this road myself, still learning what it means to devote years of one's life to questions that may never have easy answers.

<br><br>

But I promise myself this:

<br><br>

One day, I will research and write a book that tells their story.

<br><br>

A self-contained work, written from the heart, born out of experience, empathy, and truth.

<br><br>

A book not merely about research, but about researchers.

<br><br>

A book about the forgotten minds that keep the world of knowledge alive.

</div>
