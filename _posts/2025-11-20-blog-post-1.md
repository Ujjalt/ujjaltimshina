<h3><strong>Gravity</strong></h3>

<p>
This is the hour when the walls lean in<br>
A silent heavy dust-cloth gray<br>
When the clock's tick is a dying grin<br>
And tomorrow is a forgotten day
</p>

<p>
This is the weight no one else can see<br>
A stone that sits right in your chest<br>
A familiar cold anatomy<br>
That puts all other pains to rest
</p>

<p>
The world outside is a painted screen<br>
A happy play you're not cast in<br>
A dull and constant low-grade ache<br>
Beneath the skin beneath the skin
</p>

<p>
But wait a crack a slant of light<br>
Not bright or grand but soft and slow<br>
A single bird takes sudden flight<br>
A feeling you used to know
</p>

<p>
It does not shout it does not mend<br>
The heavy stone is still its guest<br>
But for a moment at the end<br>
It simply shares the weight of rest
</p>

<p>
It says the night is also part<br>
Of the slow spin of this old sphere<br>
That even broken things have art<br>
And even heavy hours are dear
</p>
