### **I Wish I Was a Dog**

I wish I was a dog  
not because life would be softer  
but because **I** would be simpler.

It is easy to say others pretend  
easy to point at noise  
and call it hollow  
but maybe it is my own voice  
that shakes a little  
when real truth is required.

Maybe I decorate my sentences too  
hoping the shine hides  
the part of me still unfinished.  
Maybe I walk into rooms  
with borrowed calm  
afraid my own quiet  
is not enough.

> I have performed certainty  
> when I was not sure.  
> I have smiled like understanding  
> when I barely held the thread.  
> And I have feared being seen  
> without all the layers.

A dog would not do that.  
A dog does not doubt  
the weight of its own presence.  
It does not rehearse honesty  
or polish its instincts.  
It does not hide behind cleverness  
when it feels small.

I wish I was a dog  
so I could stop holding myself  
to the impossible task  
of always seeming composed  
always seeming capable  
always seeming more  
than the trembling truth of me.

If simplicity were a choice  
I would choose it  
even for a single breath.
